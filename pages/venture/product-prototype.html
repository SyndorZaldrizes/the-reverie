Product Prototypes

The Reverie isn’t just a think-piece — it’s a workshop. These are messy, opinionated prototypes designed to reveal what works (and what breaks) when real people try to govern, meet, decide, and follow through.

What’s here

These tools bridge theory to practice. None are “finished.” Each is a test: can we reduce civic friction without turning governance into yet another app-shaped illusion?

Design constraint: If it can’t help someone inside a meeting, it should probably be a checklist, not a product.

Blueprint Builder

Constitutions that nobody reads and bylaws nobody understands are the default failure mode. The Blueprint Builder is a wizard-style tool that forces choices instead of letting people paste boilerplate.

  • Interactive prompts that translate legalese into plain language.
  • Warnings when you create contradictions or concentrate power.
  • Export to clean formats (PDF/Markdown) for real adoption and revision.

Awkward questions it should ask: “Who can actually call a meeting?” “How do you remove an officer?” “Why is this threshold ⅔?”

Decision-making games & apps

The analog games on the Civic Lab page are a start. But sticky notes only scale so far. These are lightweight digital tools for practice rounds when you can’t get everyone in the same room.

  • Agenda Jenga: drag-and-drop agenda setting + timers + auto debrief.
  • Budget Lab: sliders that visualize trade-offs and coalition pressure.
  • Parli Pro Speedrun: a motion trainer that rewards correct procedure like a rhythm game.

None of these replace real deliberation. They train the muscle memory so deliberation doesn’t collapse into chaos when stakes show up.

ALTIUS console integration

ALTIUS is already live as a public prototype. The venture question is whether its “catalyst” logic can be embedded without letting AI quietly take the wheel.

  • A meeting planner that nudges you into drafting the agenda before panic starts.
  • A bylaws builder that refuses to proceed until you decide removal/oversight rules.
  • A follow-through layer that turns “we should” into assignments + deadlines + accountability.

Principle: don’t let AI run your org. Let it remove the friction that stops you from doing the obvious next step.

On the horizon

These might become products, or they might die quietly after testing. Either outcome is acceptable. We’re explicit about what’s cooking.

  • A micro-survey engine: anonymous member input + clean visuals without enterprise platform overhead.
  • A civic mission tracker: link club activity to research questions (credit + data + story).
  • Open-source adapters: plug tools into LMS systems without turning schools into IT projects.
