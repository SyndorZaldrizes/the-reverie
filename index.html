What this lab actually does

Under the galaxy aesthetic there’s a simple question: how do real people, especially students and youth, experience and practice democracy?

Research

Journal memos, course papers, and long-form work on economic inequality, democratic erosion, and civic learning in the U.S. context.

Building

Translating theory into concrete things: youth-friendly explainers, campus activities, mini-curricula, and tools student orgs can actually use.

Civic Lab

A quasi-consulting arm for clubs, delegations, and small orgs that want help designing engagement blueprints, workshops, or campus experiments.

Experiments

Surveys, workshops, and field tests that measure what moves people—from apathy to action, or from confusion to “ok, I get this now.”

Featured: Campus Democratic Engagement Study

A multi-stage experiment on a college campus, tracking how messaging, peer networks, and institutional trust shape students’ willingness to engage in democratic life.

  • Combines surveys, workshops, and low-stakes simulations.
  • Connects democratic erosion theory to lived experience at the campus level.
  • Feeds back into concrete recommendations for student orgs and administrators.
