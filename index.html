About Nathan

I’m Nathan Sanchez, a student at St. Mary’s College of Maryland and Delegation Chairperson/President of our Maryland Student Legislature chapter. I spend a lot of time somewhere between campaign work, legislative simulations, and trying to explain dense political structures in plain language.

This site is my personal hub for that work: a place to collect research, design small civic tools, and document what I’m learning about democratic participation, especially for people who are usually treated as an afterthought in politics.

Current Focus

  • Democracy, civic engagement, and political socialization
  • Youth-facing political education (short-form content, explainers, games)
  • How institutions feel from the inside versus how they look on paper

Research Project: Democracy & Civic Engagement

The academic side of this project grows out of coursework, journal memos, and independent research on how democracy erodes, who participates in it, and how civic engagement is shaped by institutions, inequality, and political culture.

Core Questions

  • How do people first learn what “democracy” is supposed to mean in practice?
  • What pushes people toward participation versus disengagement?
  • How do education, media, and lived experience interact when people decide if politics is worth their time?

Outputs (In Progress)

  • Journal memos and short essays on democratic theory and erosion
  • Case-based notes from campaign and legislative work
  • Public-facing explainers that translate theory into something a younger or non-specialist audience can actually use

Youth & Digital Civic Lab

A lot of political learning now happens on phones, not in classrooms. This section will host work that connects short-form content (like TikToks and clips) to deeper explanations on topics such as constitutional rights, state structures, and policy issues.

Ideas on the Table

  • Short explainers that break the Maryland Constitution into human-sized pieces
  • Youth-facing pages that stay bipartisan but center human rights and democracy
  • Companion write-ups for videos so teachers, parents, and students can go deeper

Future Direction

Over time, this may become a structured “home base” for youth-oriented civic content: a place where media, notes, and interactive tools live together instead of floating around as disconnected posts.

Experiments & Prototypes

Not everything here will be polished. Some parts are prototypes: small tools, interactive pages, or design tests that explore how people might engage with civic content online.

Planned Experiments

  • Interactive explainers and micro-quizzes on democratic concepts
  • Early app-style flows for a civic education tool (web-first, with room to grow into a full app later)
  • Visualization of participation patterns and institutional structures

Technical Stack (For Now)

  • Static HTML, CSS, and a bit of JavaScript
  • Hosted via GitHub Pages
  • Designed to be extendable into a more app-like experience
