Methodology

Research Questions & Hypotheses

This experiment asks how universities can cultivate a culture of democratic engagement that reaches all students, including those who feel politically out of place. We draw on recent political science research showing that civic organizations can mobilize “cross‑pressured” members by emphasizing shared identity, local stakes, and norms of participation. Our core hypotheses are:

  • H1 (Framing): Messages that frame civic engagement around shared campus identity and local issues will increase students’ sense of representation and willingness to participate.
  • H2 (Norms): Messages that highlight descriptive norms — that most students participate in some form of civic activity — will boost engagement.
  • H3 (Cross‑pressure moderation): These effects will be strongest for students who perceive themselves as politically misaligned with the campus climate.

Experimental Treatments

In the pilot study, participants will be randomly assigned to read one of four short messages delivered via an online survey. Each message frames civic engagement differently: (1) a neutral control message, (2) a partisan conflict frame that emphasizes national polarization, (3) a campus identity frame that ties engagement to being a responsible member of the campus community, and (4) an identity + norms frame that combines the campus frame with descriptive norms about student participation. After reading the message, students answer questions about political efficacy, campus representation and intentions to participate in civic activities. They are also offered a real opportunity to sign up for more information or future events.

Ethics & Participation

The study has been reviewed and approved by our Institutional Review Board. Participation is voluntary, anonymous and confidential. Survey data will be analyzed in aggregate to protect individual privacy. If you decide not to participate, there will be no penalties or consequences. Participants may withdraw at any time without penalty.

If you have questions about the study’s ethics or your rights as a participant, please contact the principal investigator at research@university.edu or the IRB office at [University].
